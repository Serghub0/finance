🎓 Investment Education — Building Wealth Step by Step
Wealth is built in layers — from a protected foundation to calculated risk. This guide explains every tool in the Rosalie Financial Desk, organized from safest to most aggressive, with recommended allocation percentages for each stage.
🟢
No Risk — Capital 100% Protected
HYSA · CDs · Treasury Bills · FDIC Insured
Recommended: 20-40% ▶
What it is: Your money sits in a federally insured account earning guaranteed interest. The FDIC protects up to $250,000 per depositor per bank. Your principal can never go down.
Rosalie tool: Wealth Compounder — select "HYSA" or "CD" type. Enter your balance, APY rate, and see exactly how your money grows year by year with inflation comparison.
Current rates: HYSA pays 3.5-5.0% APY in 2026. CDs can lock in higher rates for 6-12 months. Treasury bills (T-bills) offer similar yields backed by the US government.
Real-life use: Emergency fund (3-6 months expenses), down payment savings, short-term goals (1-3 years), cash reserve while waiting for investment opportunities.
The foundation: Every wealth-building strategy starts here. You need 3-6 months of living expenses in a HYSA before investing a single dollar elsewhere. This is not optional — it's your financial safety net.
🔵
Low Risk — Stable Income
Dividend ETFs · SCHD · JEPI · Government Bonds · 401(k)
Recommended: 20-30% ▶
What it is: Investments that generate steady income with moderate price stability. Your capital can fluctuate but these assets have long track records of recovery and consistent payouts.
Rosalie tools: Income ETF Calculator — load SCHD (3.2% yield, 14yr track record) or JEPI (8.4% yield, safest covered call ETF — only dropped 3% in 2022). Wealth Compounder — select "401(k)" to model employer match + tax-deferred growth.
Key ETFs: SCHD ($65B AUM, 0.06% fee, 10%/yr dividend growth), JEPI ($45B AUM, JPMorgan managed, 8.4% yield), JEPQ (Nasdaq version, 10.8% yield).
Real-life use: Retirement accounts (401k, Roth IRA), passive income for bills, long-term wealth building with reinvested dividends. A 401(k) with employer match is the single highest-return investment available — a 100% match doubles your money on day one.
The backbone: After your emergency fund is set, this layer generates reliable income while your capital stays relatively stable. DRIP reinvestment here compounds quietly for decades.
🟡
Moderate Risk — Growth + Income
S&P 500 · QQQ · VGT · QQQI · SPYI · Real Estate
Recommended: 20-30% ▶
What it is: Broad market index funds and covered call ETFs that balance growth with income. These can drop 20-30% in bear markets but historically recover within 1-3 years. The S&P 500 has returned ~10%/yr over the last 100 years.
Rosalie tools: Income ETF Calculator — load QQQI (14.2% yield, Section 1256 tax efficient) or SPYI (12% yield, 98% S&P upside capture). Debt Freedom > Rental Property — analyze real estate deals with full Cash-on-Cash ROI and cap rate calculations. Wealth Compounder Compare — pit any two investments against each other.
Real estate: Use the Rental Property Analyzer to input actual numbers — property price, rent, taxes, insurance, vacancy rate. The calculator shows Cash-on-Cash ROI so you can compare a rental property directly against MAGY or HYSA on the same capital.
Real-life use: Core portfolio growth, retirement account holdings, real estate investment evaluation. The Buying Power calculator helps determine how much house you can afford before you even start shopping.
The engine: This layer does the heavy lifting over 10-30 years. Dollar-cost averaging into SPY or QQQ through market ups and downs has created more millionaires than any other strategy in history.
🟠
Higher Risk — Aggressive Income & Growth
MAGY · XDTE · FSELX · Sector ETFs · Individual Stocks
Recommended: 10-20% ▶
What it is: Concentrated positions in specific sectors, high-yield covered call ETFs, and individual stocks. These offer 20-40%+ annual returns but can drop 30-50% in downturns. The upside is enormous but so is the volatility.
Rosalie tools: Income ETF Calculator — this is where MAGY (37% yield, Mag 7 covered calls) lives. Use the price drift slider and event scenarios to stress-test your position. The Bear/Base/Bull chart mode shows best and worst case outcomes. DRIP 100% mode shows how share accumulation compounds weekly income over 10 years.
Key positions: MAGY (37% weekly income from Mag 7), XDTE (21% from S&P 500 0DTE options), FSELX (semiconductor growth — +62% last year), individual Mag 7 stocks (NVDA, AAPL, MSFT).
The covered call trade-off: MAGY captures ~40-60% of Mag 7 upside but absorbs ~90% of downside. The weekly income provides a thin buffer. You're trading explosive growth potential for consistent weekly cash flow. Use the Income ETF tab to model exactly how this trade-off affects your 10-year projection.
The accelerator: Only deploy money here that you won't need for 5+ years. Never invest your emergency fund or short-term savings in MAGY or sector ETFs. The income is powerful but the capital is not protected.
🔴
High Risk — Speculative
Crypto · Day Trading · Leveraged ETFs · YMAG · TSLY · Options
Recommended: 5-10% ▶
What it is: Assets with no income floor, no FDIC protection, and 50-90% drawdown potential. Crypto, leveraged ETFs, single-stock option strategies, and day trading. The potential for 100%+ gains exists alongside the potential for 100% loss.
Rosalie tools: Market Pulse > Crypto Heatmap — see which coins are pumping or dumping. Crypto Detail — CoinGecko data with 24h/7d signals. News & Sentiment — check what's driving crypto prices before acting. Wealth Compounder — select "Crypto" type to model long-term growth scenarios.
What to avoid: YMAG (70% return of capital — your own money recycled back), TSLY/NVDY (95-100% ROC), ULTY (129% yield but pure capital destruction). These look like high income but are slowly eating your principal. The Income ETF Calculator exposes this — set high yield with negative drift and watch the position erode.
Crypto strategy: Bitcoin and Ethereum are the blue chips of crypto. XRP and SOL are mid-risk. Anything smaller is pure speculation. Never invest more than you can afford to lose entirely. Consider holding crypto in a tax-advantaged account if possible.
The lottery ticket: This is play money — funded from profits, not from savings. If this entire layer went to zero tomorrow, your financial plan should survive. Scale positions only from trading profits, never from emergency funds or retirement capital.
